A useful golf outing plan starts with the group—not a complicated package. Before calling a course, decide what the day needs to accomplish, how much golf experience the players have, and how long the group can reasonably stay.

## Choose a format that fits the group

A scramble is usually the friendliest option for fundraisers, company days, and mixed-skill groups. Everyone contributes, the pace stays manageable, and newer golfers do not have to finish every hole alone.

Tee-time starts work well for smaller groups that want a familiar round. A shotgun start brings a larger field back to the clubhouse around the same time, which helps when lunch, awards, or a program follows golf.

See the available [outing and tournament options](/events) before settling on a format.

## Estimate players without waiting for a final count

You do not need every name before the first planning conversation. A realistic range—such as 48 to 60 players—helps the course recommend timing, carts, and food. Set a registration deadline that leaves room to confirm the field.

## Match food to the schedule

Boxed meals are simple before golf. Turn service keeps players moving. A buffet or casual clubhouse meal works well when the group finishes together. Review the [current clubhouse menu](/menu), then ask what can be adapted for the size of your event.

## Keep communication short and clear

Send players the arrival time, start format, basic dress expectations, and what is included. Ask them to arrive early enough for check-in and cart assignments. If weather is uncertain, designate one person to share updates with the group.
